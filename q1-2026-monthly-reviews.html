Target segments and company profiles

January was a transition month — recovering from the December holiday period while launching the next wave of campaigns. The month started with carryover campaigns from Q4 (Logistics, Innovation Labs, Chocolate n3) winding down, while six new campaigns launched from mid-January onward. The focus shifted toward Materials Manufacturing and Food Production as new verticals, alongside continued success with the chocolate gift approach.

Company sizes ranged from 50 to 1,000+ FTEs, with new campaigns increasingly targeting the 200-500 FTE sweet spot. The Netherlands remained the primary market, with Germany receiving continued attention through the Innovation Labs DE carryover. A Referral Partners outreach was also initiated for the first time, marking a new channel.

Key messaging themes

Chocolate gift campaigns continuing from Q4 (n4 carried over, n5 launched). Industry-specific pain points for Food Producers and Materials Manufacturers. ABM-driven tailored messaging for Wholesale. An experimental ERP-focused approach for Managing Directors (Rapid/Purple). First dedicated partner outreach. Compared to Q4, a clear move away from Innovation Labs/corporate targeting toward smaller, more decision-maker-dense companies.

✅ What Worked

▶
Messaging angles that drove engagement
  • Subject lines continued to perform well: "Hi {{firstName}}" and "Lleverage x {{companyName}}"
  • Chocolate gift campaigns (n4 + n5) remained the standout — phone follow-ups essential for converting interest into meetings
  • LinkedIn outperformed email significantly: 23% reply rate on LinkedIn messages vs. 1.7% on email
  • Transportation & Logistics NL (Q4 carryover) showed that longer-running sequences with multiple touchpoints eventually break through (11 email replies)
Segments that responded best
  • Food Producers (new vertical): 23 total replies despite launching mid-month. C-Level and Directors at 50-200 FTE food companies showed genuine interest
  • Materials Manufacturers 200-500 FTE: 17 replies, particularly strong LinkedIn engagement (14 of 17 via LinkedIn)
  • Logistics (carryover): Smaller companies (50-200 FTE) outperformed mid-size (200-1K FTE) — confirming Q4 learning about accessible decision-makers
Sequences that converted to meetings
  • Chocolate campaigns (n4 + n5): Dominated meeting conversion. N4 had 142 calls and n5 had 53 calls
  • Carryover campaigns: Late-stage replies converted from leads who had been in sequences for weeks
  • New campaigns: Still in early stages but showing promising reply volumes

❌ What Didn't Work

▶
Low-performing messaging
  • Innovation Labs DE: 151 LinkedIn invites + 233 emails = only 3 LinkedIn replies, 0 email replies. Diminishing returns for large (1K+ FTE) companies where innovation roles lack decision-making authority
  • Rapid - MD Manufacturers (Purple): 1 LinkedIn reply from 65 emails + 34 invites. ERP-focused messaging for MDs didn't generate traction (though launched late)
Segments with poor engagement
  • Large corporations (1K+ FTE): DMU problem from Q4 persisted — innovation roles respond but can't book
  • Wholesale 200-500 FTE (ABM): Only 8.6% LinkedIn acceptance rate — well below average
Deliverability
